Interactive Learning

Today, children are growing up in a technology-rich environment. By the time your child advances to higher learning, sitting at a desk copying figures from the board is not playing into their strengths as a student. Interactive learning is a more hands-on real world process of relaying information in classrooms. Passive learning relies on listening to the teacher or rote memorization of information. However, with interactive learning children are invited to participate in the conversation through technology or through role playing activities in class. The A 2 Zed Learning Centre has turned to interactive learning as a means of inspiring students and keeping the teacher-child relationship vital.

At the A 2 Zed Learning Centre, we offer a variety of interactive learning solutions for your child. These include:

  • Boxlight Technologies
  • Communication with parents through RENWEB SMS
  • Learning Management Systems

At the A 2 Zed Learning Centre we put the “tech” into technology. We are the place where “tech” rules.

Music, Arts and Languages

Subjects such as music, the arts and an exposure to foreign languages are beneficial to your child’s learning and development. Art develops the mind and teaches children valuable skills. Students who participate in art activities learn to think creatively and solve problems by imagining various solutions. Concentration, strong recall skills, evolved communication skills and being a good team player are just a few of the benefits research shows music and an exposure to foreign languages has on the developing mind.

At the A2 Zed Learning Centre, we offer a wide range of musical, art and language experiences for your child’s individual learning needs. These include:

  1. Music lessons that are integrated with the learning curriculum
  2. Art lessons that promote creativity and individuality
  3. Introductory Spanish lessons that broadens their horizons to communicate effectively within a diverse culture

We seek to provide your child with a means of self expression as they participate in our varied music, art and language experiences.

Physical Activities

Why are physical activities so important for your child?

Just like adults, increased physical activity has been associated with an increased life expectancy and decreased risk of cardiovascular disease. Physical activity produces overall physical, psychological and social benefits for children.

Allowing your child to participate in physical activities will help him or her to:

  • have stronger muscles and bones
  • have a leaner body because physical activity helps control body fat
  • be less likely to become overweight
  • decrease the risk of developing type 2 diabetes
  • possibly lower blood pressure and blood cholesterol levels
  • have a better outlook on life

At A 2 Zed Learning Centre, we aim to give your child a wide range of physical activities that will not only help to build his or her physical skills and coordination but also encourage increased social interaction and the development of team building skills.

  3. It also gives children a sense of movement, rhythm and an appreciation of music.
  4. Between Ages 2 to 5 they will learn to:
