Rochelle Wade-Morrison

Rochelle Wade-Morrison is the Principal/Programme Leader and a Teacher in the early learning programme at A 2 Zed Learning Centre. She gained her Bachelor of Science degree in Child Care and Development at the University of Technology, Jamaica in November 2009. Rochelle’s studies highlighted her interest in early childhood and adolescent development. She has experience working with students at the early childhood, high school and post -secondary level. She has worked within a child care centre, children’s home, high school and post-secondary college.

Rochelle had also worked as a Quality Assurance and Control Coordinator and Early Childhood Lecturer for the past two and a half years. During her tenure in her past job positions she had published an Associate Degree in Early Childhood and Adolescent Development programme. The programme had also been accredited by the City and Guilds of London Institute.

She is very passionate and involved in the early childhood field. She strongly believes that it is our daily interactions with children that help to shape who they will become in the future. It is a real joy for her to watch the children’s growth and development on a daily basis. Rochelle enjoys being a part of a network of people who are able to offer children learning opportunities and explanations about the world around them. She is grateful to be a part of the wonderful team at the A 2 Zed Learning Centre, and to work alongside the families she serves. Her hobbies and interests include swimming, reading, travelling, music and photography.

Nicole Samuels-England

Nicole Samuels-England is a trained teacher. She completed her studies at the St. Josephs Teacher’s College. Nicole has nine years teaching experience at the early childhood level. When she sees her students move from one level to the next, it brings her great joy and fulfillment. She believes she has the aptitude and potential to teach young children to read, understand and develop in a holistic manner.

Nicole understands that teaching young children requires a great deal of love, patience and understanding. Her experience has taught her that one of the first approaches in the classroom is to love her students. She understands that once students love and respect her as the teacher and the adult, then she will find herself with little or no behavioural issues in the classroom, which allows for more teaching and learning time.

Annette Parris

Annette Parris is a graduate of the Moneague College in St Ann Jamaica and holds a Diploma (with Credit) in Primary Education. She is also licensed as a Teacher with the Ministry of Education through the Joint Board of Teacher Education.

Annette served as a substitute teacher at the Holy Childhood Preparatory School in Kingston, and as a mother of two, ages 16 and 4, she brings concrete practical experience when dealing with young children. She believes firmly in the statement “Every Child Can Learn, Every Child Must Learn.” despite one’s socio- economic circumstance or ethnicity. Prior to becoming a Teacher, she served for several years in the Financial Services industry as a Customer Service Representative and Superisor, winning several awards including the Grace Kennedy Money Services Award for ‘Best Customer Service Agent’.

Annette today, is proudly part of the revolution in teaching, which recognizes and embraces the pivot role of technology in how children learn. She’s committed to the appropriate use of technology in education considers herself fortunate to be a part of the A2ZED Learning Center experience at Holy Childhood.

Myers Hamilton

Mrs. Myers Hamilton is the Chief Executive Office for the Caribbean iSchool Jamaica Limited situated in Kingston.

Caribbean iSchool was established in 2008 with its primary mandate being the delivery of on-line content in collaboration with the Jamaica Observer Limited CXC Study Series. The site attracted over 20,000 unique visitors who were preparing for the CSEC examinations. Today, with her team of experts provides the option for teachers and institutions across all sectors with the ability to customize the learning experience for their learners.

Mrs. Myers Hamilton has over 25 years of experience in areas of Information Technology, and Education combined, with a dominant accentuation in Instructional Design. She has worked on a number of research projects in the field of instructional technology (face-to-face, blended and fully online class configurations) in context of Primary and High Education.
